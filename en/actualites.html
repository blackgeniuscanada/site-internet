Around the world

They inspire us.

Verified stories of young Black people pushing the limits of intellectual excellence, everywhere in the world. We share them because at BlackGenius, this isn't the exception — it's the standard we want to build here too.

InspirationSpring 2025

International Chess Master at 14

Having arrived in the U.S. as a refugee and lived in a homeless shelter in New York, Tanitoluwa "Tani" Adewumi learned chess at school. After becoming the youngest U.S. National Chess Master at age 10, he earned the FIDE International Master title at 14.

Read on Chess.com →
InspirationMay 2023

Graduates at 13 with degrees in computer science and cybersecurity

Elijah Muhammad earned his degrees in computer science and cybersecurity from Oklahoma City Community College at just 13, already holding ten IBM certifications. He is now pursuing a bachelor's degree at Oklahoma State University.

Read on Good Morning America →
InspirationMay 2026

Youngest graduate of her class at 19

Born in Nigeria, Faithful Onilede earned her bachelor's degree in computer engineering from Binghamton University in May 2026, becoming the school's youngest graduate this year — completed in under three years thanks to high school credits.

Read on WBNG →
InspirationMay 2026

Youngest education PhD graduate at the University of Houston

After a bachelor's in psychology at 17 and a master's in education at 18, Salenah Cartier completed her doctorate in education in May 2026, becoming the youngest person to earn a PhD at the university in nearly 20 years.

Read on ABC13 Houston →
