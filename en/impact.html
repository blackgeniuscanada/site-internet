Case study · Founding family K.

"My son doubled his math average in one term."

The story of a journey that sums up the BlackGenius Canada method: an honest diagnosis, a demanding plan, regular support — and the solidarity of a community that doesn't let go.

The starting point — September 2025

The child: 11 years old, Grade 6 in a Montreal public school. Well-liked by his teachers, seen as "average" in math. First-term report card: 64% in math, 71% in science. The family was concerned but didn't know how to step in.

The child himself described himself as "not really good at math" — a classic self-fulfilling prophecy.

The group's intervention — October 2025 to June 2026

An honest academic diagnosis. A volunteer teacher from the founding WhatsApp group — the very same group that would give rise to BlackGenius Canada in 2026 — identified in two sessions that the child had a shaky grasp of three prerequisites: automated multiplication tables, long division, fractions. Not a comprehension problem: a fluency problem.

A 28-week individualized plan. 30 minutes a day, five days a week, focused on the three identified gaps. Resources shared among the group's families. A referent tutor family for when he got stuck.

A targeted competition. Preparation for Mathlon (a Canadian competition) in March, to give him a tangible goal.

Result — June 2026. Final report-card math average: 87% (up from 64% at the start). Mathlon participation score: 78/100. Enrolled in an enriched program for high school entry. Above all: the child now describes himself as "good at math."

"What changed isn't just the grade. It's that my son sees himself differently now. He knows he can do it. And that, I owe to BlackGenius." — Aïcha K., mother · Founding family · May 2026
